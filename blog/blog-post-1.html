🧠 Cognitive Distortions and Their Effects on Our Mental Health

🧩 Decoding Cognitive Distortions

Have you ever thought:
– “I’m such a failure,”
– “They must hate me,”
– “They think I’m fat,”
– “Do you even like me?”

…even when there's no real evidence behind it?

Welcome to the world of cognitive distortions — mental shortcuts that twist reality and quietly sabotage your mood, confidence, relationships, and ultimately, yourself.

What Are Cognitive Distortions?

Cognitive distortions are irrational thought patterns that reinforce negative thinking and emotions. They often surface during times of stress, anxiety, or depression — and worst of all, they feel true.

But they aren’t.
Our brains sometimes oversimplify, jump to conclusions, or assume the worst — creating a distorted version of reality that shapes how we feel and behave.

🔟 Common Types of Cognitive Distortions

  1. All-or-Nothing Thinking: Seeing things in black-and-white. “If I don’t succeed completely, I’m a total failure.”
  2. Overgeneralization: Making broad assumptions based on one event. “I didn’t get the job — nothing ever works out for me.”
  3. Mental Filtering: Focusing only on the negatives. “I made one mistake — my whole presentation was terrible.”
  4. Disqualifying the Positive: Rejecting positive experiences. “They were just being nice — they don’t really mean it.”
  5. Jumping to Conclusions: Assuming what others think (mind reading) or expecting the worst (fortune telling).
  6. Catastrophizing: Expecting disaster. “What if I panic during the interview and ruin everything?”
  7. Emotional Reasoning: Believing that feelings = facts. “I feel worthless, so I must be.”
  8. Should Statements: Using rigid rules. “I should never feel anxious. I must always be strong.”
  9. Labeling: Defining yourself/others by one event. “I’m an idiot for doing that.”
  10. Personalization: Blaming yourself for things outside your control. “They’re upset — it must be because of me.”

🌿 Why It Matters

These aren’t just “bad thoughts” — they fuel anxiety, depression, and low self-worth. Believing them shapes how we behave… and keeps us stuck.

🛠 How to Fight Back

  • ✅ Become aware: Start noticing your thoughts. Ask: “Is this 100% true?”
  • ✅ Challenge the distortion: Replace “I always mess up” with “Sometimes I struggle, but I learn and improve.”
  • ✅ Practice self-compassion: Mistakes don’t make you less worthy. Be kind to yourself.
  • ✅ Talk it out: Therapy (especially CBT) can help reframe these patterns.

✨ Final Thoughts

Your thoughts are powerful — but not always accurate. By identifying and challenging cognitive distortions, you can shift your mindset and reclaim your emotional well-being.

You are not your thoughts. You are the thinker who can change them.
So be wise… and re-think.
